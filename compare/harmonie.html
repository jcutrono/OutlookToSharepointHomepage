Save to SharePoint vs Harmon.ie

Both file Outlook email to SharePoint. They aim at different teams. Every claim about Harmon.ie below is sourced to their published documentation — links at the bottom.

Last verified: May 2026

Starting at $10/mo for 3 users. See pricing

Quick verdict

Harmon.ie is an enterprise email-management tool with a 15-user minimum and annual licensing[1]. Their published customer list emphasizes large enterprises like Canada Post and Bosch[2]. Strong fit for organizations with admin teams, annual procurement cycles, and 15+ users to license.

Save to SharePoint is built for small-to-mid teams: $10/month for 3 users, monthly billing, no minimum-seat or annual-contract requirement. Smart folder suggestions learn from how you file, with built-in batch inbox cleanup and auto-archive while away.

If your team can't make a 15-seat, $1,080/year minimum work, Harmon.ie isn't structured for you. If it can, both are real options — read the table below.

When each one is the right pick

Pick Harmon.ie when

  • Your team is 15+ users and an annual contract is normal procurement[1]
  • You want a sidebar with drag-and-drop into SharePoint sites[3]
  • You need SharePoint on-premises support (Classic edition)[4]
  • Rule-based automatic email upload via Outlook rules fits your workflow[4]
  • You're aligned with their target verticals — Government, Finance, Legal, Construction, Manufacturing[2]

Pick Save to SharePoint when

  • You're a 3- to 100-person team and the 15-seat minimum doesn't fit
  • You want monthly billing — no annual commitment
  • You want folder suggestions that learn from how you file, not rules to author
  • Batch inbox cleanup and auto-archive while away matter to your workflow
  • You want EML or PDF format options for the saved record
  • You'd rather start filing in 5 minutes than start a procurement cycle
