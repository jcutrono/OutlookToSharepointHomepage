Save to SharePoint vs MacroView Mail

MacroView Mail is the standalone email-filing product from MacroView's Microsoft 365 DMS suite. Notable for the lowest published per-user price among the enterprise options we compare. Every claim about MacroView below is sourced to their published documentation.

Last verified: May 2026

Starting at $10/mo for 3 users. See pricing

Quick verdict

MacroView Mail is part of a broader DMS family that includes DMS Pro, DMS Next, and Generator. Mail-only is $4.95/user/month with a 10-license minimum, billed annually[1]. Strong multi-device positioning — "Surfaces in Outlook on any device" — with native iOS, Android, macOS, and Windows support[2]. Sold mainly into Legal, Construction, Insurance, Property, Financial Services, Government, Energy, and Accounting verticals[3].

Save to SharePoint is built for small-to-mid teams: $10/month for 3 users, monthly billing, no minimum-seat or annual-contract requirement. Smart folder suggestions learn from how you file, batch inbox cleanup, auto-archive while away, and Save on Send — features not described in MacroView Mail's published documentation.

If you need MacroView's broader DMS capabilities (document management, generation, retention) or have a 10+ user team that wants annual licensing, MacroView's suite makes sense. For email-filing-only on a smaller team with monthly billing, Save to SharePoint is the lighter, cheaper fit.

When each one is the right pick

Pick MacroView when

  • You need a full DMS — document management beyond email — in the same vendor[1]
  • Native mobile filing (iOS/Android Outlook apps) is essential to your team's workflow[2]
  • Your team is 10+ users and an annual contract is normal procurement[1]
  • You're aligned with their target verticals — Legal, Construction, Insurance, Property, Financial Services, Government[3]
  • You want a product family with optional document generation (Generator) and migration tools[1]

Pick Save to SharePoint when

  • You're a 3- to 9-user team and the 10-seat minimum doesn't fit
  • You want monthly billing — no annual commitment
  • You want folder suggestions that learn from how you file
  • Save on Send, batch inbox cleanup, and auto-archive matter to your workflow
  • You only need email filing, not a full DMS
