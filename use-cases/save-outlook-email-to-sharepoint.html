How it works

1

Open the email

The add-in opens in the side panel. It reads the sender and subject, then surfaces a smart folder suggestion based on past saves.

2

Pick a SharePoint folder

Accept the suggestion, click a starred favorite, or browse and search any SharePoint site you can access.

3

Click Save

The email and its attachments upload directly to SharePoint. Metadata columns are populated. The add-in remembers the choice for next time.

Frequently asked questions

How do I save an Outlook email to SharePoint?

Install the add-in from the Microsoft Office store, open any email, pick a folder in the side panel (or accept the smart suggestion), and click Save. The email and its attachments upload directly to SharePoint with metadata columns filled in.

Can I save sent email to SharePoint automatically?

Yes — turn on Save on Send while composing and pick a destination folder. The email files itself the moment you hit Send.

What format are emails saved in?

EML (preserves the full email including headers — Outlook's native format) or PDF (universally readable, good for archives and audits). Set a default in settings, override per save.

Does it work in Outlook on the web and desktop?

Yes. The add-in runs in Outlook desktop, Outlook on the web, and the new Outlook for Windows. Folder rules and learned suggestions sync across all of them.

Do my emails or attachments pass through your servers?

No. Files transfer directly from Outlook to your SharePoint or OneDrive using your Microsoft 365 credentials. We never read, store, or proxy email content.

Can I use it with shared mailboxes?

Yes. The add-in works inside any mailbox you have access to in Outlook, including shared and delegate mailboxes. Each user's saves count against their own seat.
